The 4% Rule vs. Dynamic Spending: How to Ensure Your Portfolio Outlives You

For decades, the "4% Rule"—formulated by William Bengen in 1994—has stood as the gold standard for retirement withdrawals. It suggested that withdrawing 4% of your original portfolio (adjusted annually for inflation) would survive any 30-year span in modern history.

The Flaws of Rigidity

In a landscape characterized by low yields and sporadic volatility, rigid adherence to a static rule can be dangerous. A dynamic spending model, which dictates tightening the belt during bear markets and spending more flexibly during bull runs, often leads to higher lifetime withdrawal limits and lower sequence-of-returns risk.

Before deciding on a distribution strategy, you must define the "Income Bridge"—the gap between your desired lifestyle expenses and guaranteed income sources like Social Security or pensions.
