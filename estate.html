Beyond the Will: Why Living Trusts are the Cornerstone of Modern Estate Planning

The greatest misconception in wealth accumulation is assuming that a simple Will provides adequate protection for an estate. A Will explicitly goes through probate—a public, timely, and computationally expensive legal process that exposes your assets to public scrutiny.

The Revocable Living Trust

Establishing a Revocable Living Trust is the cornerstone of avoiding probate entirely. By transferring ownership of assets into the trust while you are alive, your designated successor trustees have immediate fluidity and legal standing to distribute and manage assets upon your passing or incapacitation.

Coupled with Durable Powers of Attorney and Advance Healthcare Directives, a trust builds an impenetrable wall around your family's financial infrastructure. Run your documents through our Checklist below to find your missing links.
