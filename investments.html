The Psychology of Market Cycles and Strategic Asset Allocation

The greatest threat to an investor's long-term compounding is not market volatility, but human behavior. Data consistently shows that average investors underperform the indices they invest in due to emotional mistiming—panic selling during contractions and over-allocating at market peaks.

Risk Tolerance Translates to Return

Establishing an iron-clad investment policy statement based on your mathematically calculated risk tolerance guarantees you are adequately compensated for the volatility you endure. A conservative bond-heavy portfolio (targeting 4-5% yields) protects principle but invites inflation risk. An aggressive equity growth construct (targeting 9-11%) invites temporary 30% drawdowns but secures purchasing power over decades.
